Message from the CEO

Elevators and escalators are indispensable parts of urban life that support smooth and effortless movement. Through daily research and development, we are committed to further improving your safety, quality and comfort.

System development in the not-too distant future may have functions beyond our imagination. Just as space travel was once thought to be inconceivable or fantasy-like; our persistent efforts in research and development over the years are making the unthinkable a reality. If we dream of big solutions for advancing these systems, we will achieve new methods in innovative transportation.

We have pursued the unknown and nurtured our dreams as "seeds of the future.” By nurturing these “seeds”, Fujitec has created the future of moving systems once thought to be far-reaching. In doing so, we are opening the door to a new era.

Fujitec Co., Ltd.

North American Leadership

Katsuji Okuda President

With over 35 years of leadership experience between Fujitec America and Fujitec Canada, Katsuji Okuda organizes and oversees the management of operations, engineering, manufacturing, sales and marketing and service across the United States and Canada.

Before continuing his career in America, Mr. Okuda was the General Manager of Field Business Operations for Fujitec in Japan, where he graduated from Hyogo Prefectural Sumoto Industrial Senior High School.

Joseph Rennekamp Vice President

Joseph Rennekamp started his career with Fujitec in 1992 and manages the day-to-day business operations, production, engineering and quality assurance. Joe holds a bachelor’s in Engineering from the University of Kentucky and a bachelor’s in Physics and MBA from Northern Kentucky University. He is an alternate member of ASME A17.1 Electrical Committee and ASME A17.5/B44.1.

Ray Gibson CFO and Treasurer

Ray Gibson is responsible for the financial management and consolidated reporting of activity to Fujitec Global. Mr. Gibson has an ASA in Business Administration, BSBA in Accounting from the University of Cincinnati and a MBA from Indiana Wesleyan University.

Darryl Mitchell Chief Legal Officer & Corporate Secretary

Darryl Mitchell joined Fujitec America in 2012 and serves as the CLO and Corporate Secretary. Mr. Mitchell earned his BA in Computer Science from Columbia University and his JD from the University Of Cincinnati College Of Law.

Joseph Smith New York Regional Vice President

Joseph Smith joined Fujitec in 2011 and currently manages the New York Regional operations. Mr. Smith served four years in United States Marine Corp and earned his MBA (Magna Cum Laude) and bachelor’s degree with National Honors (Alpha Phi Sigma) in Criminal Justice from Caldwell College.

Gary Krupp Southern & Western Regional Vice President

Gary Krupp has over 25 years in the elevator industry and began his career with Fujitec in 2002. Mr. Krupp graduated from the University of Florida with a bachelor’s in Business Administration/Marketing and manages the southern and western regional operations.

Tom Horan North Eastern Regional Vice President

Tom Horan began his career with Fujitec in 1995 and has nearly 50 years in the elevator industry. Mr. Horan manages Fujitec operations in the north eastern region of the United States and is a graduate of New Haven College.

Sam Patel North American Modernization Operations (NAMO) Vice President

Sam Patel started with Fujitec in 1985, and with over 40 years in the elevator industry, Sam heads up the North American Modernization Operations (NAMO). Mr. Patel also serves as an instructor for the National Elevator Industry Education Program (N.E.I.E.P.) and holds certifications as a Q.E.I. elevator inspector thru NAESA and TDLR.

History

In 1948, Fujitec began installing and servicing vertical transportation systems in Osaka, Japan. Led by founder and Honorary Chairman, Mr. Shotaro Uchiyama, Fujitec quickly became a well-respected leader within the industry expanding into Hong Kong, Korea, Singapore and Venezuela.

North America opened operations in 1977 and now services 16 metropolitan areas. Fujitec earns maintenance, modernization and installation contracts through unparalleled service of all major systems, value-added maintenance and superior technical knowledge. Our ability to identify, evaluate and manage expectations allows for a proactive method of management that keeps systems running longer with minimal downtime and out-of-pocket expenses.

Fujitec America services more than 9,500 units and has performed over 1000 modernizations over the past 10 years. Fujitec’s sustainable growth is attributed to its service-driven strategy that works to sustain the lowest number of units per mechanic in the industry. Our approach allows for increased preventative maintenance visits and decreased billable service calls over the life of the contract.
